Phase 3 · Pick the Short-List

Phase 2 handed you a ranked pool of suspicious candidates. Phase 3 picks the smartest short-list — not the top N by score, but a diverse set that covers different anomaly patterns.
Why "take top N" is not enough. Fraud rings produce bursts of near-duplicates — five transactions from one stolen card in one hour all score high. Taking the top N gives five clones of one ring and zero coverage of other rings. The review team needs diverse picks across different patterns.
The trade-off as an energy function. Every candidate gets a switch (picked Z=+1 / dropped Z=−1). Define an energy H:
  • Linear term: picking a high-S_C row lowers energy (reward for score).
  • Quadratic term: picking two similar rows raises energy (penalty for duplicates).
The short-list that minimises H maximises score while staying diverse.
How QAOA finds the minimum — five steps.
  1. Superposition: every possible short-list gets equal weight simultaneously.
  2. U_C(γ): rotate each configuration's phase by its energy → low-energy gets a distinct phase.
  3. U_B(β): mixer leaks amplitudes between neighbouring configurations.
  4. Repeat ×p layers: interference builds up on low-energy, cancels high-energy.
  5. Measure: state collapses to one concrete short-list — almost always near-minimum.
Classical exhaustive
Tries each short-list one by one. 2N combinations; N = 30 is already a billion tries.
Quantum QAOA
Holds all 2N in superposition at once. Interference biases the state toward low-energy — one measurement gives a near-optimal answer.

Optical-Fibre OTDR Traces
Photonic-fibre pool. Each row is an OTDR scan — a laser pulse sent down a fibre with back-scattered light recorded at 102 distance bins. Phase 2 already filtered for traces that don't fit the healthy-fibre profile (taps, digs, bends, breaks). QAOA now picks the confirmed-incident short-list, spread across distinct event types rather than five copies of the same tap.
Six stages: pool arrives, pairwise distances computed, start in superposition, apply alternating layers, measure, hand-off to the human review queue.

Phase 3 · QAOA Marginal Selector

Circuit

One qubit per candidate. At the start, every qubit is |+⟩ — the register encodes all 2N possible short-lists at once.

U_C(γ) rotates each configuration's phase by its Ising energy. Low-energy (high-score + diverse) configurations end up with a phase signature different from the high-energy ones.

U_B(β) mixes amplitudes between neighbouring configurations — lets the low-energy ones amplify and the high-energy ones cancel via quantum interference.

× p layers means repeating the U_C · U_B pair a few times (3 in production); each layer sharpens the amplitude on low-energy configurations.

Measurement collapses the state to a concrete bit-string (one Z_i per qubit) — the short-list Phase 3 hands to the review team.
