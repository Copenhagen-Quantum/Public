Phase 2 · Compression Test

The trick in one sentence: train the autoencoder ONLY on normal rows so it becomes very good at compressing-and-rebuilding normals. Then on any new row, if the rebuild is sloppy, that row is anomalous — because the autoencoder has never learned how to rebuild anything outside normal.

Why does "can't rebuild" imply anomaly? The autoencoder has only 3 numbers to describe any row (the latent). During training on normal data it uses those 3 numbers to encode whatever shape normals have in common. Anything else — any feature combination OUTSIDE that learned shape — literally has no home in the 3-number summary. The encoder drops the off-shape information (it has nowhere to put it); the decoder can't bring it back (it has no code for it). The missing information shows up as a mismatch between input and rebuild. Rebuild error = evidence that the row has structure the "normal shape" doesn't know about. That evidence is exactly what defines an anomaly.
Still unsupervised: during training we never say "these are anomalies." The autoencoder only sees normals. Anomalies are what it CAN'T handle at inference time — we never need to label them to find them.
Credit-card transactions — why rebuild-fails = fraud
A row's 8 features are amount, hour, merchant-risk-score, distance-from-home, device-fingerprint-similarity, etc. After training on millions of normal purchases, the encoder has found that all normal rows fit a 3-parameter pattern like {time-of-day, spend-tier, merchant-cluster}. A normal row plugs into those 3 parameters cleanly → the decoder rebuilds it exactly.

A fraud row (e.g. huge amount × foreign merchant × never-seen device at 3 AM) has a feature combination the 3-parameter normal pattern never saw. The encoder has no "code" for the weird combo — it gets rounded off during compression. The decoder rebuilds the row using the nearest normal shape, and the amount / merchant / device fields come out WRONG. Those wrong fields are the rebuild error: the autoencoder is telling you "this row contains something I was not trained to represent."
Optical-fibre OTDR traces — why rebuild-fails = intrusion
A row is a 102-bin return-loss curve. A healthy fibre gives a smooth exponential-decay shape with just 3 parameters (launch power, fibre attenuation, noise floor). The encoder squeezes any healthy trace into those 3 numbers; the decoder rebuilds a smooth decay back out — matches perfectly.

A tap / dig / bend / break creates a local spike or dip at its distance bin plus small losses downstream. The 3-parameter smooth-decay pattern physically cannot produce a spike — it has no "code" for localised perturbations. The encoder discards the spike; the decoder rebuilds a smooth decay. The rebuild fails exactly at the event's bins: the gap between input and rebuild shows you where on the cable the intrusion happened. The rebuild error is itself a diagnostic.
Classical PCA
Compresses using straight lines. Works when the "normal shape" is linear. Misses curved manifolds, so it under-flags subtle anomalies.
Quantum Autoencoder
Compresses using non-linear quantum rotations + entanglement. Catches curved normal shapes that PCA flattens out, so rebuild error is cleaner at small anomaly magnitudes.

Two Real Datasets, Side-by-Side

Credit Card Transactions
Payment-card fraud. Phase 2 receives the pool Phase 1 filtered. Each row's 8 features are compressed to a 3-value latent, rebuilt to 8, and the rebuild-error is scored. Higher rebuild error = more suspicious.
Optical-Fibre OTDR Traces
Photonic-fibre return-loss. Each row is an OTDR scan — a laser pulse sent into a fibre with back-scattered light recorded at 102 distance bins along the cable. Normal traces show a smooth exponential decay; anomalies (taps, digs, bends, breaks) create correlated spikes/dips across neighbouring bins. The autoencoder learns what a healthy fibre's decay curve looks like and flags rows that don't compress cleanly into that shape.
Six stages: see inputs, encode to latent, decode back, measure error, combine with Phase 1 score, hand off.

Phase 2 · Quantum Autoencoder

Circuit

Hardware-efficient ansatz compresses 8 qubits into 3 latent qubits. The 5 trash qubits are SWAP-tested against |0⟩₅; fidelity F is the purity of the trash register. Anomaly score S_A = 1 − F(trash, |0⟩₅). Normal rows: high fidelity, low S_A. Anomalous rows: low fidelity, high S_A.
